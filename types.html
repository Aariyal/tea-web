Tea Types

All tea comes from one plant — Camellia sinensis. The difference is in how it is processed.

Popular Varieties

Tea Type Description
☕ Black Tea (রং চা) The most common variety, often referred to locally as "রং চা" when served plain. It is typically produced using the CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl) method, which results in a strong, dark brew.
🥛 Milk Tea (দুধ চা) This is the heart of Bangladeshi tea culture. It consists of strong black tea mixed with condensed or fresh milk and sugar, often simmered until it becomes rich and creamy.
🍃 Green Tea Gaining rapid popularity for its health benefits, green tea produced in regions like Sylhet is known for its light, floral character.

Other Varieties

Tea Type Description
🫚 Ginger Tea (আদা চা।) Black tea brewed with fresh ginger, often used as a remedy for colds or for its refreshing spicy kick.
🍋 Lemon Tea (লেবু চা) A zesty, refreshing version of black tea served with lemon juice and sometimes a pinch of salt.
🧂 Masala Tea A spiced version of milk tea with a blend of aromatic spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves.
🍵 Malai Tea A premium version of milk tea topped with a thick layer of clotted cream (malai).

Special Varieties

Tea Type Description
🌈 Seven-Layer Tea (সাত রং চা) A famous innovation from Sreemangal. It is served in a clear glass with seven distinct layers of different tea concentrations and flavors.
☁️ White Tea Made from the youngest tea buds and minimally processed, this is a delicate, premium, less-commonly found tea produced in small quantities.
🫖 Oolong Tea A partially oxidized tea with a complex flavor profile, often described as having notes of fruit, flowers, and nuts. The tea is found less common for daily consumption.
